    About the Researcher

    Oleh Shynkarenko

    PhD Candidate · University of Pécs, Faculty of Philosophy

    I am a Ukrainian journalist, writer, and researcher with a lifelong passion for literature, philosophy, and science fiction. Born in Zaporizhzhya, Ukraine, my journey spans from engineering to creative writing, from journalism to academic research.

    With 20+ years in journalism, 6 fiction books published, and 600+ articles written, I bring both a practitioner’s insight and a scholar’s rigour to the study of Ukrainian speculative literature.

    Growing up where philosophy was often censored, I found intellectual refuge in Ukrainian sci-fi — works that blended profound philosophical questions with cultural resistance and visionary imagination.

    My Books

    How to Disappear Completely
    Literary Fiction · 2006
    Winner of the Smoloskyp Award. Stories where selfhood gradually unravels — spare, intimate, and faintly menacing.
    Kaharlyk
    Satirical Dystopia · 2014
    A century after a Russian-Ukrainian war. Identity, memory and time itself become unreliable in this post-apocalyptic satire.
    The First Ukrainian Robots
    Science Fiction · 2016
    A love triangle between a woman, her failing husband and a superior robot — a metaphor for what it means to be human.
    Череп
    Satire · 2017
    Three bogatyrs drive a T-34 into Ukraine, deep inside their own propaganda-fed delusion. A grotesque anatomy of imperial violence.
    Bandera Distortion
    Historical Fiction · 2019
    The space between Bandera the person and Bandera the myth — a provocation that refuses easy answers.
