# My Essays

<div class="articles-intro" markdown>
A collection of essays spanning metamodernism, film criticism, philosophy of science, and Ukrainian cultural history. Each piece blends close literary and cinematic analysis with broader social commentary — tracing how consciousness, time, memory, and meaning are shaped by the cultural moment we inhabit.
</div>

-   **[Metamodernism Manifesto](manifesto.md)**

    ---

    <span class="article-tag">Philosophy</span>
    <span class="article-tag">Cultural Theory</span>

    A philosophical manifesto arguing that metamodernism — oscillating between irony and sincerity, between trust and scepticism — has superseded postmodernism as the dominant mode of contemporary consciousness. Explores Wes Anderson's films, the Triad of Uncertainty, and why counterintuition is the defining feature of the age.

-   **[The Faces of War](faces-of-war.md)**

    ---

    <span class="article-tag">Film Criticism</span>
    <span class="article-tag">Ukrainian History</span>

    How Soviet and Ukrainian cinema shaped — and distorted — the historical memory of war. A close reading of screen performances from Dovzhenko's 1928 silent propaganda to contemporary films such as *Bad Roads*, *Cyborgs*, and *Donbas*, examining how acting, language policy, and national identity intersect on screen.

    [:octicons-arrow-right-24: Read essay](faces-of-war.md)

-   **[From Counting Angels to Counting Likes](struggle-for-reality.md)**

    ---

    <span class="article-tag">Philosophy of Science</span>
    <span class="article-tag">Epistemology</span>

    A genealogy of how positivism evolved — through logical empiricism, Popperian falsification, Foucault and Lyotard's postmodern critiques — into the post-truth era of social media, deepfakes, and AI-generated knowledge. Science as religion, and its inevitable successor.

    [:octicons-arrow-right-24: Read essay](struggle-for-reality.md)

-   **[Metamodern Oscillation in *You Are the Cosmos*](metamodern-oscillation-ostrykov.md)**

    ---

    <span class="article-tag">Film Analysis</span>
    <span class="article-tag">Metamodernism</span>

    Pavlo Ostrikov's 2024 Ukrainian science-fiction film analysed through six competing interpretive frameworks: courtly love, the "Million Red Roses" motif, Kubrick's *2001*, existentialism, Lacanian psychoanalysis, and geopolitical allegory. A demonstration of metamodern hermeneutics in practice. *In Ukrainian.*

    [:octicons-arrow-right-24: Read essay](metamodern-oscillation-ostrykov.md)

-   **[Portrait of a Russian Intellectual as a Crocodile](portrait.md)**

    ---

    <span class="article-tag">Cultural Critique</span>
    <span class="article-tag">Soviet History</span>

    How the beloved Soviet cartoon characters Crocodile Gena and Cheburashka — originally a gentle satire of the Marxist intelligentsia — became icons of Russian imperialist propaganda deployed in the war against Ukraine. A study in the weaponisation of childhood nostalgia.

    [:octicons-arrow-right-24: Read essay](portrait.md)

-   **[Metamodern Timescapes: The Changing Image of Time](timescapes.md)**

    ---

    <span class="article-tag">Philosophy of Time</span>
    <span class="article-tag">Media Studies</span>

    From Newton's absolute time and Einstein's relativity, through McTaggart's idealist paradox, to the digiphrenia and narrative collapse of the smartphone era. A survey of how our relationship with time — mediated by cathedral bells, wristwatches, television, and Telegram — has transformed across four cultural epochs.
